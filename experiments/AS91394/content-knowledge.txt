Content‑Knowledge for AS91394 – Analyse Ideas and Values of the Classical World
Contexts: Socrates │ The Aeneid │ Augustus

1. Socrates (Classical Greek philosophy and dialogue)
- Context may include Plato’s Apology, Crito, Phaedo, or dialogues exploring Socratic ethics and civic responsibility. Though not frequently chosen, these texts allow thematic focus on leadership, identity, power.  :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

2. The Aeneid (Roman epic by Virgil)
- Most commonly used context in candidate responses. Frequently selected Books: 1, 2, 4, 6, and later books such as Book 12 are also effective. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
- Key ideas: pietas (duty), destiny, leadership, cultural identity, empire‑building.
- Supports use of Latin terminology and range of examples from across the text.

3. Augustus (Augustan political leadership and ideology)
- Context often drawn via convergence of *The Aeneid*, Virgil’s imperial themes, and historical sources (e.g. Res Gestae, Suetonius). :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
- Enables exploration of leadership, propaganda, national identity, ideological values.
- Requires clear anchoring of literary analysis in historical context.

WIDER IDEA & VALUE THEMES (NZQA Specification note 3):
- Social relationships & role of the individual
- Leadership & heroism
- Power & freedom
- Social & cultural traditions
- Ideological & religious beliefs
- Ideals of behaviour & national identity
- Literary conventions
- Influence on other cultures  :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

ADDITIONAL NOTES:
- Exemplar responses succeed when socio‑historical context is integrated into textual analysis, not just described. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
- Use of Greek or Latin terminology, appropriately explained, enhances depth of analysis. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
